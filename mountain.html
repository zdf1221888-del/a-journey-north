MOUNTAIN

Mountains have always carried a sense of quiet reverence. Perhaps because I grew up in the south, the mountains of the north feel different to me — more distant, more solemn, and somehow more powerful in their presence. Under a blanket of winter snow, the landscape takes on an added sense of mystery and distance. When the low winter sun illuminates the slopes, the ridges of the snow-covered peaks become sharply defined, revealing a character that feels steady. These photographs attempt to capture that balance — the quiet strength and stillness of northern mountains in winter.
